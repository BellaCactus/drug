meta understanding

drugs can be understood in 3 layers:

structure → what it is chemically (shape, rigidity, substitutions)
receptor → what it binds to (5-HT2A, dopamine, μ-opioid, etc.)
effect → what it feels like (visuals, stimulation, sedation, delirium)

interesting note: if you know structure + receptor, you can usually predict the effect pretty well

psychedelics / phenethylamines

2C-E

potent psychedelic phenethylamine with duration around 8–12 hours, longer than 2C-B and generally more intense
acts as a 5-HT2A receptor agonist, producing strong visuals, deep introspection, and a heavier, more analytical headspace
less euphoric and more mentally demanding compared to 2C-B, with more pronounced body load (tension, nausea in some cases)
visuals tend to be sharper and more geometric, with stronger alteration of perception and thought patterns

interesting note: often described as a more serious, less “fun” version of 2C-B

Bromo-DragonFLY

extremely potent benzodifuran psychedelic with duration typically 18–36+ hours
strong 5-HT2A receptor agonist with vasoconstriction effects
rigid structure increases binding and slows dissociation

interesting note: feels “locked-in” due to structural rigidity

2C-B

moderate duration (4–8 hours)
visuals, euphoria, tactile enhancement
more social and manageable than LSD

interesting note: halfway between MDMA and LSD

opioids

Fentanyl

μ-opioid receptor agonist
rapid brain penetration due to lipophilicity

interesting note: baseline reference for extreme potency

Carfentanil

~100x fentanyl potency
extremely small doses active

Ohmefentanyl

multiple isomers with varying potency
tiny structural differences massively change strength

stimulants

MDPV

dopamine/norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor
extreme stimulation and potential psychosis

Desoxypipradrol

very long duration stimulant
effects can last 24–72+ hours

deliriants / anticholinergics

Scopolamine

blocks acetylcholine receptors
causes true delirium and confusion

Diphenhydramine

normal antihistamine
at high doses causes delirium

chemistry patterns

benzodifurans → rigid phenethylamines → longer duration
thio substitution → heavier effects, altered metabolism
halogens → potency and binding changes
n-substitution → massive potency shifts (especially opioids)
