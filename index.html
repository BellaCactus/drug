▶ psychedelics / phenethylamines 8 compounds
⬡
2c-e.png
2C-E
  • potent psychedelic phenethylamine with duration around 8–12 hours, longer than 2C-B and generally more intense
  • acts as a 5-HT2A receptor agonist, producing strong visuals, deep introspection, and a heavier, more analytical headspace
  • less euphoric and more mentally demanding compared to 2C-B, with more pronounced body load (tension, nausea in some cases)
  • visuals tend to be sharper and more geometric, with stronger alteration of perception and thought patterns
often described as a more serious, less "fun" version of 2C-B, with stronger cognitive depth and intensity
⬡
bromo-dragonfly.png
Bromo-DragonFLY
  • extremely potent benzodifuran psychedelic with duration typically 18–36+ hours, sometimes longer in toxic cases due to slow metabolism
  • acts as a very strong 5-HT2A receptor agonist, with additional peripheral serotonin activity causing vasoconstriction (blood vessel narrowing)
  • rigid benzodifuran structure increases receptor binding affinity and slows dissociation, meaning it stays active much longer than flexible molecules
  • physical risks include vasoconstriction, numbness, and potential tissue damage in severe cases
feels like a "locked-in" phenethylamine because the structure physically limits flexibility, so it doesn't disengage easily
⬡
2c-b.png
2C-B
  • moderate duration psychedelic (4–8 hours) acting primarily as a 5-HT2A receptor agonist
  • produces visuals, euphoria, tactile enhancement, and mild stimulation
  • relatively low receptor binding strength compared to stronger psychedelics, which is why it's shorter and more manageable
  • less intense cognitive disruption compared to LSD, making it more social and "recreational"
often described as halfway between MDMA (body/euphoria) and LSD (visuals)
⬡
2c-g.png
2C-G
  • rare long-duration 2C compound lasting up to ~30 hours
  • likely acts on serotonin receptors similarly to other 2C compounds but with slower metabolism or stronger binding
  • produces extended mild-to-moderate psychedelic effects without the same intensity peak as shorter compounds
basically the same "2C idea" but stretched across a full day
⬡
2c-t-7.png
2C-T-7
  • psychedelic phenethylamine with thio (sulfur) substitution, affecting metabolism and receptor interaction
  • acts on serotonin receptors but with more body load and variability in effects
  • associated with nausea, vasoconstriction, and unpredictable responses at higher doses
sulfur substitution changes how the molecule interacts with the body, often making it heavier and less predictable
⬡
dob.png
DOB
  • long-lasting psychedelic amphetamine (~18–30 hours)
  • combines 5-HT2A agonism with stimulant effects from amphetamine backbone
  • amphetamine structure slows metabolism and increases duration and stimulation
  • produces strong visuals, stimulation, and long plateau phase
adding an amphetamine backbone dramatically increases duration compared to 2C compounds
⬡
aleph-1.png
Aleph-1
  • obscure compound from Shulgin's work with limited human data
  • likely acts similarly to other psychedelic amphetamines with long duration and strong effects
  • not well studied, but known to be intense and long-lasting
one of the deeper PiHKAL entries that shows how far the chemistry was explored

  • part of the trimethoxyamphetamine family

  • less potent visually but still structurally important

▶ benzodifurans deep dive
what is benzodifuran?

type of chemical structure, basically a scaffold. benzo = benzene ring, di-furan = two furan rings (5-member rings with oxygen). so benzodifuran = benzene fused to two furans → creates a rigid, locked shape

rigidity makes them act differently than normal phenethylamines

why rigidity matters
  • normal phenethylamines (like 2C-B) are flexible, can wiggle and rotate, bind then let go
  • benzodifurans (like Bromo-DragonFLY) have fused rings → can't rotate, already shaped "correctly" for receptor
  • instead of "bend into shape" → "already in perfect shape"
rigid = stronger binding, longer effect

base phenethylamine structure

benzene ring + ethyl chain (–CH2–CH2–) + amine (–NH2 or similar). flexible, metabolized fairly quickly

this is why normal 2Cs are shorter acting

benzodifuran changes

side chain loops back into ring, two furans fused onto benzene. 3 main effects:

  • rigidity ↑ → stiff, precise receptor fit
  • receptor binding ↑ → stronger 5-HT2A interaction
  • metabolism ↓ → harder for enzymes to break down
all 3 together = long, intense effects

why Bromo-DragonFLY lasts so long
  • high receptor affinity → binds strongly
  • slow dissociation → doesn't let go
  • rigid structure → resists breakdown
effects don't fade normally, they just... continue

compare to normal 2Cs
  • 2C-B → flexible, short, weaker binding
  • 2C-B-FLY → semi-rigid, longer, stronger binding
  • Bromo-DragonFLY → fully rigid, extremely long, very strong binding
rigidity stacking = extreme duration

FLY vs DragonFLY
FLY = "kinda locked"
DragonFLY = "fully locked boss form"

why tiny changes matter
  • receptors are 3D
  • tiny tweaks (add ring, swap O→S, add F) → massive effect
  • can change potency, duration, metabolism
chemistry is wild, a tiny atom swap can break or enhance everything

mental model
  • flexible molecule → shorter, softer
  • rigid molecule → longer, stronger
  • more receptor affinity → more potent
  • slower metabolism → longer duration
▶ analog patterns (general concept)
what is an analog?

an analog = a compound that's chemically very similar to another but with small changes. small tweaks can massively change: potency, duration, toxicity, receptor selectivity

tiny atomic swap or side chain change → completely different effect

how analogs happen in practice
  • phenethylamines / psychedelics: 2C-B → 2C-E → 2C-G → all small substitutions on same base ring. FLY vs DragonFLY → adding fused rings changes rigidity/duration. halogen substitutions (Cl, F, Br) → change potency and visuals. thio substitutions (O → S) → heavier, more body load
  • tryptamines / LSD analogs: LSD → AL-LAD → ETH-LAD → small tweaks on the indole ring or N-substitutions. changes headspace: visuals vs emotional vs cognitive effects
  • opioids: fentanyl → acetylfentanyl → carfentanil → ohmefentanil → etc. N-substitutions, halogens, thio vs oxy → potency skyrockets or drops. carfentanil ~100x fentanyl, ohmefentanil even higher
  • stimulants: MDPV → α-PVP → desoxypipradrol → methiopropamine. small substitutions on phenyl or pyrrolidine ring → longer duration, more erratic effects
  • deliriants / anticholinergics: scopolamine → other tropane analogs → similar delirium but different potency/duration. Diphenhydramine → similar antihistamine analogs → normal med vs full delirium at high dose
  • misc / experimental: benzodifurans: 2C-B-FLY → 3C-B-FLY → Bromo-DragonFLY. NBOMe/NBOH: 25I-NBOMe → 25B-NBOMe → 25E-NBOH. small substitutions = active at micrograms vs milligrams
"analog explosion" is why research chemicals are so unpredictable

factors changed by analogs
  • potency → binds receptor stronger/weaker
  • duration → metabolism slowed by rigidity, halogens, or ring fusion
  • toxicity → small change = huge increase in risk
  • psychological profile → visuals, euphoria, headspace can shift
  • physical profile → vasoconstriction, body load, stimulation, sedation
"analog" doesn't just mean similar—it's like a sibling with a totally different personality

mental model for all compounds

base structure = starting point. analog = tweak on that structure. tweak types:

  • ring fusion (rigidity)
  • halogen substitution (F, Cl, Br)
  • thio/oxy swap (S ↔ O)
  • N-substitution (fentanyl, tryptamines)
  • alkyl/ethyl tweaks on indole or phenyl rings

result = new potency, duration, toxicity, and effects

with fentanyl analogs, a microgram difference can be the line between "high" and death; with psychedelics, a single atom tweak can flip visuals/emotional weight
▶ chemistry patterns (my understanding)
  • benzodifurans — "rigid phenethylamines that bind longer and last way longer"
  • thio substitution — "swap oxygen for sulfur → changes metabolism and feel, often heavier"
  • fluoro/chloro — "add halogens → changes potency, binding, and duration"
  • n-substitution — "small nitrogen changes → massive potency differences, especially in opioids"

overall analog patterns (summary)
  • ring fusion → more rigidity → longer duration
  • halogen substitution → changes potency + binding
  • thio substitution (O → S) → heavier effects, different metabolism
  • N-substitution → massive potency shifts (especially opioids)
  • amphetamine backbone → longer duration + stimulation
  • NBOMe modification → extreme potency increase
meta understanding

drugs can be understood in 3 layers:

  • structure → what it is chemically (shape, rigidity, substitutions)
  • receptor → what it binds to (5-HT2A, dopamine, μ-opioid, etc.)
  • effect → what it feels like (visuals, stimulation, sedation, delirium)
if you know structure + receptor, you can usually predict the effect pretty well
